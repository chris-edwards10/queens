Queens Leads City's Shelter Population

By: Christopher Edwards - Dec. 22, 2024

More than 26,000 indivudals are living in shelters in the Queens, according to recently released data from the Department of Homeless Services (DHS), making it the borough with the highest population in shelters. Brooklyn follows with 23,000 and the Bronx has more than 20,000 living in shelters. Queens community district 12, which includes the neighorhoods Jamaica, Hollis, St. Albans, Springfield Gardens, Baisley Park, Rochdale Village, and South Jamaica has the highest number of people in shelters of any district in the city, with 8,997 living in shelters as of Ocotber 31.
